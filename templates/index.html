Access to Health.

Every Refugee has the right to access the health services provided by the National Health Service (NHS)

You can access the following for free: General Practitioners (GP), Hospitals, Maternity Services

Employment

You have the right to work as soon as you have been granted refugee status in the UK

Finding a job will not only increase your income but will help you settle into your new life.

Access your Benefits

Benefits are monetary payments from the government to help you and your family live in the UK.

You should do this straighaway

Safety

You and your family should always feel safe

You can contact the police on 999 for emergency or 101 foe less urgent situations.

Housing

You have the right to have somewhere to live.

There are two types of housing in the UK: Private housing or Social Housing.

Access to Education

Education is important in the UK and there is a strong culture of lifelong learning.

Adult learning is strongly encouraged and many different courses are available

Legal Rights

Ev ery person in the UK has the same basic human rights and freedoms, which are protected in law.

Empowerment

You should feel empowered becasuse your voice, choices and autonomy are fundamental human rights.
